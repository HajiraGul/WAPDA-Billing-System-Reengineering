# WAPDA Billing System Re-Engineering Case Study

## 📖 Project Overview
This project presents a comprehensive case study on the re-engineering and modernization of the Water and Power Development Authority (WAPDA) billing system in Pakistan.

The study analyzes the legacy billing infrastructure, identifies operational inefficiencies, and evaluates the technological transformation undertaken to improve accuracy, transparency, and efficiency.

---

## 🏢 About WAPDA
The Water and Power Development Authority (WAPDA) is a major public-sector organization responsible for managing water and power infrastructure in Pakistan. Due to increasing demand and billing-related complaints, modernization of its legacy systems became necessary.

---

## 🎯 Objectives of Re-Engineering

- Modernize billing processes
- Minimize human errors
- Improve data integrity
- Enhance reporting and analytics
- Reduce operational costs
- Improve customer transparency

---

## ⚠️ Problems in Legacy System

- Manual meter readings
- Estimated billing inaccuracies
- Overbilling disputes
- Paper-based record management
- Fragmented databases
- Legacy technologies (COBOL, FoxPro, Batch Systems)
- Poor scalability and automation

---

## 🔄 Technology Stack

### Before Re-Engineering
- COBOL
- FoxPro
- Batch Processing Scripts
- Non-relational localized databases

### After Re-Engineering
- Java & .NET
- Web-based customer portals
- SQL Server / Oracle databases
- Power BI & Crystal Reports
- Smart Meters & Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI)

---

## ✅ Outcomes & Benefits

- ~90% reduction in billing errors
- Faster revenue collection cycles
- Increased transparency
- Improved customer satisfaction
- Enhanced data security
- Better regulatory compliance
